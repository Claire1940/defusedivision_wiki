<Section icon={Target} title="Post Better LFG Messages and Get Faster Replies">

Good LFG messages are short, specific, and easy to scan. The more time someone spends decoding your post, the less likely they are to answer. Focus on the details that affect team fit: region, language, role preference, and whether you use voice chat.

You can also use the server to ask for strategy help, but keep the request narrow. “Any tips?” is too broad. “Best way to hold a bombsite with randoms?” gives people a direction and usually gets better responses.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Safety, Moderation, and Scam Checks">

Discord is useful, but open servers also attract fake giveaways, phishing links, and account traps. If a message sounds urgent or too generous, slow down. Most scams rely on pressure, not logic. A good rule is simple: do not click unfamiliar files, do not share credentials, and do not trust unofficial “reward” pages.

Moderation also matters. Stay polite, avoid flooding channels, and report rule-breaking posts through the proper path. That keeps the server readable for everyone and helps the moderators spend time on real issues instead of cleanup.

<Callout type="warning" title="Red Flags to Watch">
Any message asking for your login, browser session, token, or password should be treated as unsafe. Legitimate moderators do not need private credentials.
</Callout>

| Red Flag | Why It Is Risky | Safe Response |
|---|---|---|
| “Free reward” DM | Common phishing lure | Ignore, block, report |
| Fake verification link | Can steal account access | Verify only through trusted channels |
| Untrusted download | Possible malware | Do not install it |
| Pressure to act fast | Removes your judgment window | Pause and confirm |
| Ask for your password | Never needed for support | Refuse immediately |
