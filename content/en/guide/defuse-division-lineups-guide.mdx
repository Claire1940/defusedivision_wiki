<Section icon={Target} title="Attack Lineups for A Site, B Site, and Mid">

Attacking lineups work best when you treat the map like three linked problems: how to enter, how to split attention, and how to finish the plant safely. Since Defuse Division uses standard bomb-site structure, the safest offensive setups are usually the ones that control a chokepoint first and then convert that pressure into a site hit.

When you build an attack plan, keep the focus on sightlines and timing windows. Do not overcommit to a deep push if your team still has a better play through Mid or a second lane. Repeatable pressure beats forced hero plays more often than not.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid This Mistake">
Do not memorize a lineup without checking the actual geometry. A set play only works if your crosshair, movement, and entry timing match the route.
</Callout>

| Attack Phase | Goal | Best Lineup Habit | Common Error |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Round | Take space safely | Hold a stable angle before moving | Sprinting into first contact |
| Mid Round | Split defenders | Sync two lanes at the same time | Arriving one by one |
| Site Hit | Break anchor setup | Clear close corners first | Planting before control is secured |
| Post-Plant | Protect the bomb | Reposition to a safe crossfire | Watching the same lane twice |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pick the entry lane">
    Choose A, B, or Mid based on where your team has the best timing and the fewest exposed angles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assign one trade partner">
    Every front-line player should have someone close enough to punish the defender who wins the first duel.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Force the anchor back">
    Pressure the main choke long enough that the defender cannot stand on the site with full confidence.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Plant from cover">
    Use a position that protects the planter and gives your team a clean post-plant angle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A useful rule is to think in terms of “lane, trade, and finish.” If your team owns the lane, the trade is ready, and the plant location is safe, the attack lineups are already doing their job. That same structure also keeps your team from wasting time on empty space.

For more structured route planning, the official Roblox page and game-facing update posts are better references than random community rumors. As of 2026, the game’s public stats also suggest an active player base, which is one reason consistent set plays remain valuable in live matches.

<Callout type="info" title="Attack Priority">
If the round starts with bad spacing, slow the push and rebuild the trade chain. A delayed but organized hit is usually stronger than a rushed collapse.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Defense Lineups, Crossfires, and Retake Paths">

Defense is where lineups become discipline. On defense, your job is to stall the push, gather information, and survive long enough for a teammate to arrive. The best setups are usually the ones that create a crossfire or force attackers to clear multiple angles at once.

This is where bomb-site awareness matters most. You want one anchor that can slow the entry, one support position that can punish the follow-up, and one rotation lane that is not blocked by overpeeking. If you lose map control too early, your retake becomes harder before the bomb is even planted.

| Defensive Role | Positioning Goal | Best Use | Avoid When |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anchor | Hold the first contact point | Early round stall | You are alone with no backup |
| Support | Cover a second angle | Punish wide swings | The site is already collapsed |
| Rotator | Arrive late and clean up | Retake or reinforce | You leave site too early |
| Flank Watch | Prevent free rotations | Stop enemy split plays | Your team already has full map info |

<Callout type="success" title="Defense Win Condition">
A successful defense lineup does not need to win instantly. It only needs to buy time, force errors, and keep the retake realistic.
</Callout>

If you are playing a defensive hold, the most important habit is resisting the urge to fight every angle alone. A crossfire is stronger than a solo peek because it makes the attacker choose between two threats. That choice is what creates easy trades.

The other half of defense is the retake. Once the plant goes down, your lineup should shift from holding space to reclaiming it in order. Clear the nearest danger first, then the site body, then the likely post-plant angle. Rushing the bomb without support usually hands the round back.

A practical defensive mindset looks like this:
- Hold one stable line instead of wandering.
- Save health for the retake if the site is already lost.
- Rotate with purpose, not panic.
- Communicate when you give space so teammates know the timing.

The official game framing also supports this style of play: Defuse Division is built around bombs, timers, and team coordination, so defense lineups are strongest when they are patient and structured.

<Callout type="warning" title="Defensive Trap">
Do not stack too many players on one angle early. If the attackers hit the other side, your rotation may arrive too late to matter.
</Callout>

</Section>
