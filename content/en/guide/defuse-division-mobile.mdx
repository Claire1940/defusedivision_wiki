<Section icon={Settings} title="Touch Controls and HUD Tuning">

The biggest advantage on mobile comes from removing friction. If your thumbs need to travel too far, your aim becomes inconsistent and your reaction time suffers. Build a layout that lets you move, look, and interact without fighting the interface.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Lock the core controls">
    Keep movement, camera, and the main action buttons close together so your hands never leave the screen center for long.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Separate fire and interact">
    Make sure your attack or action button does not sit directly beside your interact button, or you will mis-tap during tense moments.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reduce HUD clutter">
    Hide anything that blocks sightlines, especially around the center and lower-right areas where mobile players usually look most often.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Test in low-pressure rounds">
    Run a few practice matches or casual sessions and adjust sensitivity until you can track targets without overcorrecting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Control Element | Best Mobile Choice | Common Mistake |
|---|---|---|
| Movement stick | **Left edge, fixed** | Placing it too low and losing control |
| Camera swipe zone | **Large and open** | Crowding it with buttons |
| Interact button | **Easy reach** | Overlapping it with fire |
| Utility slot | **Visible but secondary** | Making it the largest button on screen |
| Reload or swap | **Near the thumb path** | Hiding it behind menus |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
If your aim feels shaky, lower sensitivity first before changing layout. A cleaner swipe range usually improves consistency faster than a full UI rebuild.
</Callout>

Mobile players do best when they play the round in stages instead of forcing every fight. Treat each round like a series of decisions: get information, hold space, trade safely, and only commit when the map state is favorable.

<Callout type="warning" title="What Not to Do">
Do not chase every sound cue on your own. Mobile inputs are less forgiving than mouse and keyboard, so over-peeking usually creates free openings for the other side.
</Callout>

A good round plan on defuse-style maps is usually simple:

- Take the safest route with the fewest exposed angles.
- Hold the angle you can actually maintain on a touch screen.
- Reposition only when your team can cover the move.
- Save utility for the moment it changes the round, not the first possible second.

When in doubt, choose the decision that keeps you alive longer. On mobile, surviving a little longer often creates better trades, better information, and better late-round control.
