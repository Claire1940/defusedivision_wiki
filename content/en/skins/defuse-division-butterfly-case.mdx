<Callout type="tip" title="Settings First">
Before grinding matches, spend a few minutes on controls, sensitivity, and visibility. A small tweak can be worth more than a new loadout.
</Callout>

The home page notes that players can open the main menu with **N** and the team-select menu with **M**. That makes the setup process simple, and it also means new players should verify their menu flow before jumping into live rounds.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open the main menu">
    Use **N** to check sensitivity, audio, and any available visual options.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Set team flow">
    Use **M** to understand how team selection works before the round begins.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stabilize your aim">
    Pick a sensitivity that lets you track corners without overshooting common angles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Test visibility">
    Make sure enemy silhouettes, HUD elements, and crosshair contrast are easy to read.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Keep it consistent">
    Lock in one setup for several matches before changing it again.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Setting Area | What to Tune | Good Starting Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Sensitivity | Mouse speed or stick responsiveness | Stable tracking, not wild flicks |
| Audio | Master and effects volume | Hear footsteps, swaps, and objective cues |
| Visibility | Brightness, contrast, UI clarity | Spot threats without eye strain |
| Key flow | Menu access and interaction habits | Faster team selection and cleaner prep |

| Problem | Likely Fix | Why It Helps |
|---|---|---|
| Missing easy shots | Lower sensitivity slightly | Reduces overcorrection |
| Losing track of enemies | Raise contrast or calm the visual clutter | Improves target separation |
| Slow round starts | Learn the N and M menu flow | Saves pre-round time |
| Misclicking objective actions | Rebind or practice interaction timing | Lowers input mistakes |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid This">
Do not keep changing your setup after every loss. One bad round does not prove your settings are wrong.

<Section icon={Map} title="Map Control and Route Priorities">

<Callout type="warning" title="Map Discipline">
Most tactical mistakes happen when players overcommit to one lane and forget the rest of the map. Watch the choke points, then rotate with intent.
</Callout>

The strongest map habits in Defuse Division are simple: hold space, deny easy entries, and avoid giving up mid control for free. Even without a public map glossary, the standard structure is clear enough to build reliable habits around bomb sites, chokepoints, and rotation routes.

| Map Area | What It Does | What You Should Do |
|---|---|---|
| Bomb site | The main objective zone | Clear angles before planting or entering |
| Chokepoint | Narrow entry path | Use it to slow pushes and force fights |
| Mid control | Central routing space | Hold it long enough to predict rotations |
| Rotation route | Faster side-to-side movement | Use it only when the round state justifies it |

A good round plan usually looks like this:

| Phase | Attack Plan | Defense Plan |
|---|---|---|
| Early round | Take space together | Gather info, hold key lanes |
| Mid round | Probe for weak angles | Delay, bait utility, keep site safe |
| Late round | Commit only after a numbers edge | Rotate fast if the pressure shifts |

The safest way to learn a map is to repeat the same path several times and notice where you die, where you get spotted, and where your team loses contact. That feedback loop matters more than memorizing ten flashy routes.

| Common Mistake | Better Habit |
|---|---|
| Solo rushing a choke point | Move with a teammate and trade |
| Rotating too early | Wait for clearer information |
| Staring at one angle too long | Clear angles in a planned order |
| Ignoring objective sound cues | React to the round clock and map pressure |

<Callout type="success" title="Strong Habit">
If your team has map control, slow the round down. If the enemy has map control, stop guessing and recover space together.
</Callout>

</Section>
