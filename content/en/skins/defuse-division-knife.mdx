<Section icon={Shield} title="Defuse Division Knife Basics">

The **Defuse Division knife** is a situational tool in a Roblox tactical shooter built around bomb sites, rotations, and fast punish windows. In practice, the knife matters most when the round is already shaped by positioning. If you are already in control of space, the knife can help you move decisively, finish a weak target, or save time between safe angles.

| Situation | Knife Value | Best Action |
|---|---|---|
| Clear rotation | High | Sprint through safe lanes and keep moving |
| Isolated duel | Medium to High | Close the gap only if the target is trapped |
| Open site entry | Low | Stay on your main weapon and clear first |
| Post-fight cleanup | High | Swap in for the finish if no threats remain |

<Callout type="tip" title="Editor’s Rule">
Treat the knife like a timing weapon. If the position is already won, the knife speeds up the round. If the position is uncertain, it usually creates risk instead of value.
</Callout>

A good knife round is rarely flashy. It is controlled, efficient, and short. The strongest players do not chase knife kills for style; they use the melee slot to preserve momentum, reduce hesitation, and punish opponents who overextend into bad spacing.

<Section icon={Map} title="Movement, Crossings, and Close-Range Control">

Knife play becomes stronger when your movement is deliberate. That means using the weapon around safe corridors, broken sightlines, and short crossings rather than wide-open lanes. The goal is not to “be faster” in every situation. The goal is to arrive with better timing than the opponent expects.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Read the lane before you move">
    Check whether the next angle is open, covered, or contested. The knife only adds value when the route is already under control.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Use safe space for the swap">
    Pull the knife during rotation windows, not while pushing into an unknown stack. Safe space is where the knife saves time.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Close distance with purpose">
    If you commit to a knife chase, commit with a clear line, a trapped target, or a teammate forcing attention elsewhere.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Re-equip before the fight widens">
    The best knife users are disciplined about swapping back early. Once a duel can turn into a multi-angle threat, survival matters more than speed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Good knife movement is less about aggression and more about confidence in the route. When you know the path, the knife trims dead time. When you do not know the path, it becomes a liability that hands the enemy an easy punish.
