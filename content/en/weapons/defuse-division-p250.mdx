<Section icon={Map} title="Angles, Crosshair Placement, and Site Control">

The P250 rewards short, decisive fights. That means your most important skill is not fancy movement; it is pre-aiming the right body height and holding the right line. In Defuse Division-style play, the best pistol duels usually happen around choke points, bombsite entrances, and fast rotation paths. If you enter those spaces with your crosshair already placed, the P250 feels much stronger.

| Range | Best Habit | What to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Close | Hold head or upper-chest level | Panic spraying at the floor |
| Mid | Pre-aim the corner before peeking | Wide-swinging without info |
| Long | Take only disciplined taps | Challenging a rifle head-on |
| Retake | Move with a teammate | Solo peeks through open lanes |

| Map Area | Why It Matters | P250 Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Chokepoints | Forces direct contact | Hold tight and punish timing mistakes |
| Mid control | Opens rotation options | Peek only with cover or backup |
| Site entrances | Common duel zones | Clear one angle at a time |
| Rotation routes | Create flank pressure | Stay quiet and use surprise value |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid the Trap">

</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Settings That Make the P250 Easier">

A good pistol does not fix a bad setup. If your mouse sensitivity feels floaty, your frames drop during fights, or your crosshair disappears into the background, the P250 becomes harder to use than it should be. The cleanest setup is usually a stable one: moderate sensitivity, readable crosshair, and graphics settings that keep input smooth.

| Setting | Recommended Start | Why It Helps |
|---|---|---|
| Sensitivity | Moderate, then tune in small steps | Makes taps and micro-adjustments steadier |
| Crosshair | Small, static, high-contrast | Easier to keep on target during fast peeks |
| Graphics | Low to medium | Reduces stutter in fights and rotates |
| Audio | Loud enough for footsteps | Helps you pre-aim before contact |
| Menu Shortcuts | Learn the main menu and team menu keys | Saves time between rounds |

The official game information also points out that **N** opens the main menu and **M** opens team selection, so it is worth building those shortcuts into your routine if your current build uses them. Small navigation speedups matter more than most players think.

<Callout type="success" title="Stability Wins">
If your aim feels inconsistent, lower visual clutter before you chase a new sensitivity. A clearer screen and steadier FPS often improve pistol performance faster than constant settings changes.
</Callout>
